# Bidding

## The bid

After cards are dealt, each player bids how many tricks they expect to win this round.

- Valid bids: **0** through **hand size** (number of cards dealt).
- Bids are simultaneous (in person) or sequential in digital play.
- Your bid is secret until revealed, except:

### Bids above 5

At the physical table, bids **greater than 5** are spoken aloud. GUB may surface high bids for table awareness.

## Why bidding matters

Scoring rewards **exact** bids. Missing your bid costs points even if you won tricks. See [Scoring](./06-scoring.md).

## Zero bids

Bidding **0** means you intend to win **no tricks**.

- Exact: **+10 × hand size**
- Miss (win ≥1 trick): **−10 × hand size**

Zero bids are high-risk, high-reward in later rounds when hand size is large.

## Harry the Giant (artifact)

When **Pirate Abilities** are enabled, winning a trick with Harry lets you adjust your bid by **±1** for scoring purposes. See [Pirate abilities](./08-pirate-abilities.md).
